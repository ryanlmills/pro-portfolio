PWP Milestone 1

Purpose, Audience, & Goal

Purpose: This project will be a professional portfolio website highlighting both front-end and back-end web development.

Audience: This website has been designed primarily with web development and IT professionals in mind; however, it will also provide an example for non-professionals and students. The target audience is broadly made up of hiring managers, recruiters, recruiters, peers, and web development students/hobbyists.

Goal: The site's goals are to serve as a public-facing representation of my work and establish contact with users for the sake of employment, collaboration, and education through multiple avenues of communication provided.

Persona

Name: Leanna Zamora

Age: 37

Profession: Recruiter for Placeholder.io, a small startup seeking a full-time, entry-level front-end web developer. They specialize in enterprise, web, cloud, and mobile development projects.

Technology: Advanced Mac user. Recent MacBook Pro between home and office, iPhone 11.

Attitudes and Behaviors: Her job is her life, frequently tasked with extra duties that don't quite fit her job description. As long as a resume passes basic muster for her, she'll dig into a portfolio extensively enough to try to get a feel for personality and work ethic in addition to skills.

Frustrations and Needs: Leanna has gone through a series of candidates with decent enough resumes but lackluster portfolios. Her time frame is tight but she can't afford to make the wrong hire either. She is hoping that the option of remote work will balance out the moderate pay and benefits. Due to the remote nature of the position, the interview will be paramount in helping her find a self-starter.

Goals: Leanna needs to see a portfolio that displays the right personality in addition to technical skill.

User Story: As a user, I need to find work that displays both professionalism and a creative, meticulous standard of work.
